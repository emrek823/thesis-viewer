# Pharma Platform Switching Costs Lock In Veeva/IQVIA—Field Force Reduction Creates Revenue Headwind by 2028

## 1-2 Sentence Summary

Large pharma is locked into Veeva/IQVIA platforms because switching CRM with 2,000-person field forces requires 1+ month training and creates data migration risk → only justified for "game-changing" technology. BUT per-rep licensing creates revenue headwind as field forces shrink 20-30% post-COVID, forcing platform consolidation and likely M&A between Veeva and IQVIA.

---

## Investment Take

**Today:**

The consensus says Veeva is a SaaS compounder with healthcare moat. The contrarian view: **per-rep licensing is a structural vulnerability.** Field force reductions directly translate to license reductions because Veeva prices per-rep-per-license ($1.2-1.5M annually for large pharma like Actelion/Janssen).

Three dynamics create the headwind:

1. **Field force reduction is structural** — "20-30% reduction post-COVID" as pharma shifts to digital engagement. Fewer reps = fewer licenses = declining revenue per customer.

2. **Switching costs are real but work both ways** — Changing CRM with 2,000-person field forces requires 1+ month training. BUT once locked in, pharma can't leave even if dissatisfied. "Only justified for game-changing technology." This creates customer captivity, not customer love.

3. **Platform consolidation is inevitable** — "Pharma consolidates to fewer vendors rather than piecing together best-of-breed because integration complexity and duplicate costs outweigh modularity benefits." IQVIA (data + CRO) and Veeva (software + EDC) are complementary. Expert thesis: "One will acquire the other or merge."

**The mechanism:** Per-rep licensing ties revenue to headcount → field force reductions create structural headwind → switching costs prevent churn but don't prevent revenue decline → platform consolidation + M&A becomes the growth strategy.

**The winner:** Marketing automation and MSL programs expand as field reps decline → digital engagement channels (email, marketing automation) consolidated with CRM. Medical science liaisons become primary HCP touchpoint, driving Veeva CRM adoption for tracking.

**In 3-5 years:**

- **Winners:** Veeva (if they successfully shift to value-based pricing), IQVIA (if they acquire Veeva or merge). Digital engagement platforms that embed into CRM.
- **Losers:** Per-rep-licensed point solutions. Salesforce (lacks pharma-specific workflow depth).
- **Market structure:** Veeva + IQVIA consolidation likely. 2-3 pharma platforms dominate (down from 5+).

## The Counter-Thesis

The strongest counter-argument is that Veeva can successfully transition to value-based pricing (per-patient, per-trial, or platform fees) to decouple from field force headcount.

**Response:** Pricing transitions are difficult at scale. Veeva's 2,000+ pharma customers are accustomed to per-rep pricing. Transitioning creates short-term revenue risk and requires significant sales/CS investment. The transition may take 3-5 years, during which the headwind persists.

For this thesis to be wrong: Veeva would need to announce and successfully execute a pricing model transition that grows revenue per customer despite declining field forces.
