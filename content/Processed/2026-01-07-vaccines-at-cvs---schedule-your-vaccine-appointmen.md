## Learn more about your vaccine schedule

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends different routine vaccinations for different age groups. Explore the schedule information per age group below or visit the [CDC immunization schedule page](https://www.cdc.gov/vaccines/hcp/imz-schedules/index.html) for more information on which vaccines are recommended.

## Vaccines offered at CVS

### COVID-19 vaccine

The CDC recommends the 2025/26 COVID-19 vaccine for:

* **Adults 65 and older:** Decision-making for vaccination is individually based.
* **People 6 months\* to 64 years at increased risk:**Vaccination decisions are individually based and need-focused, emphasizing that vaccination is most beneficial for individuals who are at an increased risk for severe COVID-19 disease and less so for individuals who are not at an increased risk. The Centers of Disease Control and Prevention lists specific COVID-19 risk factors but also leaves the list open-ended, for people to consult with their medical provider or pharmacist regarding any other conditions or situations that can create a higher risk.

Conditions that put you at higher risk, according to the CDC, include, but are not limited to:

* Being a past or current smoker
* Being physically inactive
* Blood disorders (including sickle cell disease)
* Body Mass Index greater than 25
* Cancer
* Chronic liver disease
* Chronic lung disease, including cystic fibrosis
* Dementia or a neurologic condition
* Diabetes
* Heart disease, including high blood pressure
* HIV or tuberculosis infection
* Kidney disease
* Mental health conditions
* Pregnancy
* Solid organ or blood stem cell transplant
* Substance use disorders
* Weakened immune system
* Any other conditions or situations that place you at high risk of severe illness from COVID-19 (consult with your pharmacist or medical provider if you are unsure)

\*Subject to state law, COVID-19 vaccines for ages 5+ are available at select CVS Pharmacy® locations. COVID-19 vaccine for ages 18 months+ are available at all MinuteClinic® locations**.**

### Flu vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends an annual flu vaccination for nearly everyone 6 months of age or older, and our certified immunizers are able to vaccinate patients as young as 3 years old in some states. Get your flu shot to help reduce your risk of serious complications from the flu, including hospitalization.

* [Get flu vaccine information](/immunizations/flu?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-flu)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-flu)

### Hepatitis B vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends the hepatitis B vaccine for children and adolescents younger than 19 years of age, adults ages 19 through 59 who have not been vaccinated, adults ages 50 years and older with risk factors for hepatitis B infection, as well as infants. For infants born to hepatitis B negative mothers, the CDC recommends individual-based decision-making to decide whether to administer the hepatitis B vaccine at birth or begin the series later in infancy.

* [Get hepatitis B vaccine information](/immunizations/hepatitis-b?cid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-hepb)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-hepb)

### HPV (human papillomavirus) vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that the HPV vaccine be administered to individuals as young as 9 years old and those up to 26 years of age or if recommended by your health care provider.

* [Get HPV vaccine information](/immunizations/hpv?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-hpv)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-hpv)

### Meningitis (meningococcal) vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that all 11 to 12 year olds get a MenACWY meningococcal vaccine with a booster dose at 16 years old. Those between 16 and 23 years old also may get a MenB meningococcal vaccine. The CDC also recommends a meningococcal vaccination for other children and adults who are at increased risk for meningococcal disease.

* [Get meningitis vaccine information](/immunizations/meningitis?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-meningitis)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-meningitis)

### MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that children get two doses of MMR vaccine, starting with the first dose at 12 to 15 months of age, and then getting the second dose at some time between ages 4 and 6. Teens and adults should also be up-to-date on their MMR vaccination.

* [Get MMR vaccine information](/immunizations/mmr?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-mmr)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-mmr)

### Mpox (formerly monkeypox) vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends an mpox vaccination for any person with known or suspected exposure to mpox and for specific populations that may be at higher risk of exposure to the mpox virus. Visit the [CDC web page on mpox](https://www.cdc.gov/mpox/vaccines/?CDC_AAref_Val=https://www.cdc.gov/poxvirus/mpox/vaccines/index.html) to learn more about the vaccine. Call your local CVS® for vaccine availability.

[Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup.html?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-mpox)

### Pneumonia (pneumococcal) vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends this vaccine for:

* Adults 50 years of age or older
* Patients 19 through 49 years of age or older who have certain medical conditions or risk factors

* [Get pneumonia vaccine information](/immunizations/pneumonia?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-pneumonia)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-pneumonia)

### Polio vaccine

To help protect children from the effects of a polio infection, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that children get four doses of polio vaccine, each at the following stages: 2 months of age, 4 months, 6 through 18 months, then at ages 4 through 6 years.

* [Get polio vaccine information](/immunizations/polio?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-polio)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-polio)

### RSV (respiratory syncytial virus) vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that the following adults get one lifetime dose of the respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) vaccine to provide protection against serious RSV illness:

* People 75 years or older
* People between 50 and 74 years old and at increased risk of severe RSV (have certain chronic health conditions or live in a nursing home)

Women who are 32 through 36 weeks pregnant during September through January are recommended by the CDC to get one dose of maternal RSV vaccine to help protect their babies.

* [Get RSV vaccine information](/immunizations/rsv?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-rsv)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-rsv)

### Shingles vaccine

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends two doses for two groups:

* Adults ages 50 and older
* Adults ages 19 and older if they have a weakened immune system from disease or therapy

* [Get shingles vaccine information](/immunizations/shingles?icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-info-shingles)
* [Schedule vaccinations](/scheduling/patient-lookup?lob=rximz&flow=vaccine&icid=immunizations-lp-zone3-schedule-shingles)

### Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria and whooping cough) vaccine

The Tdap vaccine can help prevent tetanus, diphtheria and pertussis (whooping cough).
