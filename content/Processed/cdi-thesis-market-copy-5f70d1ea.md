## Slide 1: CDI & CODING SIT WITHIN MID-CYCLE RCM AND ITS PROCESSES TAKE PLACE THROUGHOUT AN INPATIENT STAY

### Front Revenue Cycle

*   **Patient Access**
    *   Patient Scheduling
    *   Patient Pre-registration
    *   Patient Registration / Admitting
    *   Customer Relationship Management
    *   Customer Service
*   **Coverage Identification / Preauthorization**
    *   Primary health insurance eligibility check
    *   Secondary / tertiary health Insurance eligibility check
    *   Liability insurance eligibility check
    *   Special payment program eligibility check
    *   Insurance enrollment and application
    *   Pre-authorization / certification
    *   Referral management
*   **Patient Financial Engagement**
    *   Patient out of pocket cost estimation
    *   Propensity to pay assessment
    *   Point of service (POS) collections
    *   Establishment of payment plan
    *   Patient financial counseling (e.g. payment plan)
    *   Charity care evaluation

### Mid-Revenue Cycle

*   **Care Management**
    *   Utilization review
    *   Disease management
    *   Case management
    *   Discharge planning
    *   Care coordination
*   **Encounter Coding & Charge Capture**
    *   Charge capture
    *   Charge review / reconciliation
    *   Clinical documentation improvement
    *   Coding
    *   Charge master management and review
    *   Recovery audits (RAC)
    *   Billing

*   **Claims Processing and Clearing**

    *   Claim reconciliation

    *   Payor follow ups, e.g. appeals management
    *   Coverage discovery
*   **Patient Billing and Collection**
    *   Claim and debt validation

## Slide 5: HOW WE ARE DEFINING THE CDI MARKET

### MARKET DEFINITION

*   Offerings aimed at improving the accuracy and speed of clinical documentation development by
    *   Having clinical professionals (e.g., CDI nurses and/or physicians) review and validate clinical documentation against care and services provided
    *   Bundling procedures as needed to accurately reflect acuity and the corresponding DRGs
    *   Distilling large amounts of physician notes to be easily translated into code by coders
*   Focus on CDI will increase significantly in light of the shift to the value-based care reimbursement model

## Slide 20: 3. RISK BASED MODELS HAVE LIMITED IMPACT ON RCM VALUE CHAIN, BUT INCREASED RELIANCE ON HIGH QUALITY DOCUMENTATION AND CODING

### CHANGES DUE TO RISK BASED PAYMENT MODELS

*   **Agreement**
    *   A relationship is struck between providers and payers including patient attribution, covered services, and estimated medical costs
*   **Billing and claims**
    *   Providers code, bill, and follow up on claims as they do today under a FFS model
*   **Analysis**
    *   The payer and provider each review medical costs to see what, if any, savings were achieved
*   **Payout**
    *   Payer pays provider organization bonus based on savings achieved
*   **Bonus distribution**
    *   Provider organization divides bonuses among program provider participants (e.g., hospital, specialist, primary care, etc.

*   Determining eligibility of patients is more important (critical to know which patients are within a particular managed population and risk pool)
*   Increased emphasis on top notch mid-cycle performance (e.g., exhaustive documentation, charge capture) to ensure risk is accurately captured

There is minimal change in the way RCM service providers function except for a renewed emphasis on documentation and coding excellence

## Slide 31: 4. GROWTH ADJACENCY: COMPUTER-ASSISTED PHYSICIAN DOCUMENTATION (1/2)

### WHAT IS CAPD?

*   **Definition:** CAPD is the continuous real-time analysis of documentation that prompts physicians with suggestions to help drive towards greater specificity required for coding of a patient encounter
*   **Role of Tech vs. Service:**
    *   Services currently exist either in-house through CDI programs (concurrent) or second level review and audits via third party vendors (retrospective) to improve physician documentation behavior. CAPD seeks to automate this review and prompt physicians in real-time at the point of care/documentation.
*   **Fit in RCM Value Chain:**
    *   CAPD's aim is to enhance documentation at the point of care, before it funnels into other elements of the mid-cycle RCM process flow (e.g., CDI, coding, etc.)
    *   Concurrent nudges help providers document accurately while engaged in a patients' care vs. revisiting documentation retrospectively

### MARKET

*   **Size and Growth:**
    *   **U.S. Spend, $MM:**
        *   2020: 75-125
        *   2025: 200-250
    *   **CAGR:** +17-23% p.a
    *   **Note:** Current market size likely under-represents total potential opportunity size given nascency of CAPD space
*   **Market Trends and Growth Drivers:**
    *   Increasing rate of denials (>20% in the last 5 years)
    *   Increasing complexity of coding, changing medical necessity and pre-authorization rules across states and payers, which drives need for more accurate documentation
    *   Increasing focus on cost and upstream improvements in RCM value chain pushes stakeholders to think about how to improve documentation at the source rather than further downstream with the CDI team
    *   Rise of VBC contracts that pushes providers to achieve a specificity and accuracy of documentation for HCC coding
    *   Physician burnout via administration tasks increases demand for concurrent and efficient processes at the point of care
