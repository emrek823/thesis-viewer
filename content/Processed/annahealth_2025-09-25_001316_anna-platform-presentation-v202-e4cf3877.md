### Commentary (2)

## Slide 7: US HEALTHCARE INDUSTRY

**US HEALTHCARE INDUSTRY**
Administrative complexity costs practices up to 35% of their revenue and requires more administrators than clinicians to operate.

### Administrative Functions, FTEs, and % of Revenue

| Function                       | Description                                                     | FTEs Required (Per 10 Providers) | % of Revenue |
| :----------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------- | :----------- |
| Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) | Includes billing, coding, and collections                       | 2 - 3                            | 6 - 10%      |
| Eligibility & Benefits Checks  | Verifying patient insurance + authorizations                    | 1 - 2                            | 3 - 5%       |
| Scheduling & Rescheduling      | Appointment setting, cancellations, optimization                | 1 - 1.5                          | 2 - 4%       |
| Patient Intake & Registration  | Collecting forms, uploading ID/insurance                        | 0.5 - 1                          | 1 - 2%       |
| Clinical Documentation Support | Transcription, note cleanup, EMR entry assistance               | 1 - 1.5                          | 3 - 5%       |
| Overall Admin Overhead         | Overarching staff ops, training, compliance                     | 1 - 2                            | 5 - 7%       |
| Contracting & Credentialing    | Negotiating payor contracts, enrolling providers with insurers | 0.5 - 1                          | 1 - 2%       |

### Commentary

*   Up to 35% of practice revenue is spent on non-clinical administrative tasks
*   This materializes in full-time staffing of up to 12 people for every 10-providers, greater than the number of clinicians
*   Tasks such as eligibility checks, prior authorizations, and scheduling are predominantly manual, leading to increased labor costs and potential for errors
*   RCM activities, including billing and coding, can consume a significant portion of practice resources, constituting 6%+ of revenue alone
*   Delays in provider credentialing and contracting with payers can postpone revenue generation, affecting cash flow
