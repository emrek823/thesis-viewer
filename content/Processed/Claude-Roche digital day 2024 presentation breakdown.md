## Roche gRED Digital Day 2024: Breakdown for Healthcare VCs

### The Headline

Roche's November 25, 2024 investor day showcased how Genentech (gRED) is operationalizing AI/ML through its **"Lab-in-the-Loop"** paradigm—a framework where computational models and wet lab experiments continuously reinforce each other to accelerate drug R&D.

---

### Key Themes from the Presentation

**1. The Core Problem: Drug R&D Productivity Crisis**

Roche opened by framing the fundamental challenge: drug R&D has a chronically low success rate. The presentation introduced a framework for R&D productivity improvement across:
- **Volume** (more candidates)
- **Probability of Technical Success (PTS)** (better candidates)  
- **Cycle Time** (faster iteration)
- **Costs** (efficiency)

**VC "So What":** This framing matters because it signals Roche isn't treating AI as a science project—they're explicitly tying it to the ROI metrics that matter to investors. The presentation outlined how they're using AI to enhance R&D productivity across volume, revenue, PTS, costs, cycle time, effectiveness, and efficiency.

---

**2. Lab-in-the-Loop: The Operating Model**

The "Lab-in-the-Loop" is described as "a mechanism by which you bring generative AI to drug discovery and development." The paradigm works as follows:

- **Data → Model → Prediction → Experiment → Data** (continuous cycle)
- AI models generate hypotheses about molecules or targets
- Automated high-throughput labs test predictions
- Results retrain and refine models

One model using this approach was "about 54-fold better compared to the original approach, which relied on expert medicinal chemists to select the most promising molecules to synthesize."

**VC "So What":** The 54x improvement metric is notable. If validated at scale, this represents potential order-of-magnitude improvements in lead optimization efficiency. The key question: Can this translate to clinical candidates?

---

**3. SCimilarity: Cell Atlas Foundation Model**

A major technical highlight was **SCimilarity**, a deep-metric-learning foundation model published in *Nature* (November 2024). Key capabilities:

SCimilarity enables "rapid queries of tens of millions of cell profiles from diverse studies for cells that are transcriptionally similar to an input cell profile or state."

- Trained on 7.9 million cells
- Can search a reference atlas of **23.4 million cells** across 412 scRNA-seq studies
- Can compute pairwise similarity across 2.5 million profiles "in 2 seconds"

The tool was validated by using macrophage profiles from interstitial lung disease as queries—SCimilarity "identified similar cells across datasets in a matter of seconds, revealing shared states in fibrotic diseases, COVID-19, and various cancers."

**VC "So What":** This is infrastructure for target discovery at scale. By finding the same pathological cell states across diseases, you can identify shared mechanisms and potentially repurpose drugs or discover new targets. The Nature publication adds scientific credibility. For VC portfolio companies building on single-cell data, this is the competitive bar from large pharma.

---

**4. Strategic Technology Partnerships**

The presentation emphasized Roche's partnership strategy:

**NVIDIA Partnership**
The alliance will bring "AI, the lab, and the clinic together to uncover otherwise inaccessible patterns in vast quantities of data, and to design experiments to test those patterns."

**Recursion Collaboration**
"The scale of this project is almost unheard of. We are screening libraries of small molecules in parallel with genetic perturbations and RNA profiling approaches, so we'll have an immediate path forward with potential medicines."

**VC "So What":** Roche is buying vs. building for compute infrastructure while investing heavily in proprietary biology tools. Portfolio companies selling AI/ML tools into pharma need to understand this dynamic—big pharma wants differentiated biological insights, not commodity compute.

---

### The Bigger Strategic Picture

Drawing from broader analysis, Roche's AI strategy rests on three pillars:

**1. Integrated Pharma-Diagnostics Model**
Roche's "Diagnostics division, which has a vast global installed base of instruments. In 2023 alone, these platforms performed 29 billion tests worldwide." This generates proprietary training data.

**2. Data Moat via Acquisitions**
- **Flatiron Health**: Real-world oncology data from ~3.4 million patient records
- **Foundation Medicine**: 800,000+ genomic profiles, 1.3 million+ clinical reports

Roche's approach enables them to "model how a patient with a specific rare genomic alteration (from FMI data) responds to a novel therapy versus standard of care over a five-year period in a real-world setting (from Flatiron data)."

**3. Platform Strategy**
Through navify® Digital Pathology, Roche adopted "a model analogous to the tech industry's most successful platforms, like Apple's App Store," inviting third-party AI developers to integrate their algorithms.

---

### The Flatiron Complication

The Flatiron acquisition has created competitive friction: "Once Roche acquired them no other company wanted to give a competitor data." Roche's 2024 finance report includes a "CHF 3.2 billion goodwill impairment charge related to Flatiron Health and Spark Therapeutics."

**VC "So What":** The Flatiron impairment is a cautionary tale about captive data strategies. RWE companies in your portfolio should think carefully about strategic acquirer dynamics—pharma ownership can destroy the value proposition for other pharma customers.

---

### Investment Implications

| Theme | Implication for VC Strategy |
|-------|----------------------------|
| **Lab-in-the-Loop maturation** | AI-native biotechs competing with gRED need clear differentiation—either more unique data or novel modalities |
| **Foundation models in biology** | Infrastructure plays (cell atlases, protein models) face competition from well-resourced pharma internal efforts |
| **Compute partnerships** | NVIDIA/AWS relationships suggest big pharma won't build compute—but will aggressively build proprietary bio models |
| **RWE complexity** | Neutral/independent positioning may be more valuable than pharma acquisition for RWE companies |
| **Multimodal data fusion** | The real moat is linking genomics + clinical outcomes + diagnostics—rare capability |

---

### Bottom Line for Healthcare VCs

Roche's presentation demonstrates that **AI in drug discovery is entering operational maturity** at the top-tier pharma level. The 54x improvement metric in molecule selection and the 2-second cell atlas queries represent real capability, not vaporware.

**Competitive positioning question**: Is your portfolio company building something that fits *into* a pharma AI infrastructure (point solution), or something that could replace/compete with these integrated capabilities (platform play)? The bar just got meaningfully higher for the latter.
