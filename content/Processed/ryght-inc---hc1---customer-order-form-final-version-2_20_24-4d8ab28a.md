## STATEMENT OF WORK

All hc1 users shall receive access to Ryght to securely use generative artificial intelligence for day-to-day use.

In addition, Ryght and hc1 shall work together to deploy into production the Disease Research and Patient Cohort Copilot mostly completed in 2023. hc1 and Ryght will make every effort to deploy in hc1 production within Q2 of 2024 and Ryght shall not invoice for additional services to put this into production.

*   Users shall be able to rapidly classify common disease states, associated lab tests and write code search hc1's database ("Disease Research and Patient Cohort Copilot"). The copilot is intended to allow users to cohort patients in a fraction of the time compared to legacy methods. Authentication and Security changes necessary to embed the copilot securely within hc1 are included.
    *   disease or therapeutic area classification
    *   lab test compendium and logical observation identifiers names and codes (LOINC) identification
    *   SQL generation specific to hc1 schema

Ryght and hc1 shall work together to enhance and deploy additional generative AI use cases on a case by case basis. hc1 and Ryght will agree to commercial terms that take into consideration hc1's business model and Ryght's cost to support usage. The hc1 and Ryght teams will agree on the next use case before proceeding with the development and will create and agree upon a project scope and milestone timeline to govern the project. The scope and timeline will define any ongoing development changes that will be implemented by Ryght in support of the use case.

All applications developed on the Service and sold to hc1 customers shall incur base subscription plus usage fees payable to Ryght, which will be agreed on a case by case basis. Ryght will provide ongoing development support and consultation during the implementation period. hc1 developers shall have access to Ryght's developer platform to build additional generative artificial intelligence I use cases and copilots.
