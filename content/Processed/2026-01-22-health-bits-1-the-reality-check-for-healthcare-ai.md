### Scaling AI Faster than Healthcare Workflows

In the U.S., abundant capital pushed AI quickly into clinical and operational workflows, from documentation and triage to billing and care coordination.

### Clinical Rigor and Infrastructure-first Execution

Europe has taken a different path. **Less rush, more scrutiny**. Regulation and market structure forced a slower pace, and that has become an advantage for more sustainable healthcare AI deployment.

AI is gaining traction first in well-defined settings like private care and specialized clinics, where workflows are clearer and integration is easier. Beyond these niches, the focus has stayed on **clinical rigor, data quality, and regulatory alignment**.

System-level pressures are shaping adoption, from workforce shortages to access constraints. Rather than scaling broadly, many European teams are aligning AI with specific clinical problems, following a more deliberate, **infrastructure-first path to execution**.
