## Slide 10: Selecting Our Targets

1.  High level "management" team in place
2.  Limited outsourced operations
3.  No customer with >15% of total revenue
4.  Geographic or subspecialty specialization

## Slide 11: What A Billing Business Gives Us

*   **Reduced dependence on payers and managed care organizations**
*   **Sandbox to innovate and experiment with new technology enabled initiatives**
*   **Direct channel to engage independent doctors including the ones we are already working with.**
*   **Tenure / being able to say we have been doing the same thing for decades.**
*   **Cashflow.** Always cash-flowing asset with typical EBITDA margins between 20% to 80%

## Slide 12: 4 Steps to Integrate a Billing Business

We are a technology enabled services business. And we are operate in technology enabled services settings. We'll take our playbook that we've used for the various groups within Apollomed and apply it to whatever billing business we decide to purchase.

*   Augment Administrative and HR Support to retain key employees and customers.
*   Map out all workflows and identify areas to streamline operations.
*   Integrate technology and operations we have developed + purchase best of breed software (where applicable).
*   Change Management + Retool. Build Build
