# Thesis: AI-Native Underwriting Unlocks SMB Stop-Loss Market — Value Capture Contested by 2028

## Mechanism

[AI VENDORS + MGUs] substitute [EXTERNAL POPULATION DATA] for [MISSING CLAIMS HISTORY] because [SMALL EMPLOYERS LACK 2-3 YEARS DATA] → [SMB MARKET EXPANDS FROM 13% TO 44% LEVEL-FUNDED PENETRATION]

---

## Investment Take

**Take:** AI-native underwriting doesn't predict catastrophic claims better—it unlocks previously unaddressable SMB market segments by substituting external data for missing claims history. The market expansion is confirmed (level-funded grew from 13% in 2020 to 44% in small firms by 2025), but value capture between technology vendors and large carriers remains contested.

**Market dynamics:** Stop-loss is a $35B+ annual premium market growing 15% CAGR to $113B by 2034. The shift to self-funded plans (now 67% of covered workers) drives demand. But carrier profitability is under pressure—Cigna missed Q4 2024 earnings on stop-loss losses, Voya raised premiums 21%+, loss ratios worsened from 78.5% to 84.1% (2017-2022).

**Problem:** Traditional underwriting requires 2-3 years of claims history, excluding small employers. Small groups (<100 lives) were "simply not possible to quote before" according to Medical Risk Managers CEO.

**Why now:** KFF 2025 confirms 44% of workers in small firms (10-49 employees) are now in self-funded/level-funded plans, up from 37% in 2024. Premium increases of 9%+ for 2026 (highest in 15 years) accelerate employer flight from fully-insured.

**AI impact:** First-order: AI substitutes external population data to enable underwriting without claims history. Second-order: Carriers consolidate (Nationwide-Allstate $1.25B, July 2025) to achieve scale. Third-order: Large carriers (UnitedHealth with 2,000+ AI engineers, Optum AI Marketplace) may internalize AI capability, squeezing vendor TAM.

**In 5-10 years:** Market consolidates to 5-7 dominant carriers. MGU layer either acquired or becomes embedded infrastructure. The contested question: Does Gradient AI get acquired for $500M+ (bull case) or marginalized as carriers build internally (bear case)? Gene therapy carve-outs ($2-4.5M per treatment) emerge as separate risk pools. Level-funded reaches 50%+ penetration in small groups.

## Bull Case

* **AI enables underwriting without claims history** — "Until now, we were not able to offer these plans to smaller businesses due to the lack of claims history. Now with Gradient AI's SAIL, we can quote business that was simply not possible before" — Medical Risk Managers CEO (*2025-12-21-AI-for-stop-loss-insurers*)
* **Level-funded penetration accelerating** — 44% of workers in small firms (10-49 employees) now in self-funded/level-funded, up from 13% in 2020 ([KFF 2025](https://www.kff.org/health-costs/2025-employer-health-benefits-survey/))
* **Carrier consolidation validates scale thesis** — Nationwide acquired Allstate stop-loss for $1.25B, closed July 2025, gaining 13,000+ SMB relationships ([Nationwide PR](https://news.nationwide.com/nationwide-completes-acquisition-of-allstate-employer-stop-loss-business-for-125-billion/))
* **Gradient AI winning new MGU customers** — Evolution Risk Partners (Jan 2025), Connexure partnership (Oct 2025), Western Skies, ATS Underwriting continue using SAIL ([Gradient AI](https://www.gradientai.com/))
* **Market quadrupling** — Stop-loss $27B (2024) to $113B (2034) at 15.1% CAGR ([Allied Market Research](https://www.insurancebusinessmag.com/us/news/breaking-news/stop-loss-insurance-market-expands-as-employers-seek-protection-against-highcost-claims-558346.aspx))

## Bear Case

* **Large carriers building AI internally** — UnitedHealth has 2,000+ AI engineers, 1,000+ AI use cases, launched Optum AI Marketplace (June 2025) selling AI to other carriers. Craig Kurtzweil: "At our core, we're a data company" ([Becker's Payer](https://www.beckerspayer.com/payer/at-our-core-were-a-data-company-unitedhealth-leans-on-ai-to-drive-growth/))
* **95% AI pilot failure rate** — MIT State of AI in Business 2025 reports 95% of generative AI pilots fail. Insurer satisfaction with AI tools dropped from 22% (2023) to 1% (2025) ([Insurance Edge](https://insurance-edge.net/2025/12/22/the-hype-trap-ai-agents-cant-deliver-the-certainty-insurance-demands/))
* **Level-funded regulatory risk** — DOL, HHS, Treasury (July 2023) expressed concerns about stop-loss bypassing consumer protections. NY, CA, DE restrict stop-loss for small employers ([Fenwick](https://www.fenwick.com/insights/publications/the-shifting-regulatory-landscape-for-level-funded-plans-an-alternative-for-group-health-insurance))
* **Carrier profitability crisis** — Cigna Q4 2024 earnings missed on stop-loss losses. Voya announced 21%+ premium increases. Loss ratios worsened to 84.1% ([Healthcare Brew](https://www.healthcare-brew.com/stories/2025/05/21/stop-loss-insurance-medical-cost-crisis))

**The counter-thesis:** If large carriers (UnitedHealth, Cigna) internalize AI underwriting as table stakes AND the MGU layer gets squeezed through consolidation, then technology vendor TAM shrinks dramatically. Gradient AI's 118 employees cannot compete with UnitedHealth's 2,000+ AI engineers building Optum AI Marketplace. Value stays with capital providers, not intelligence layer.

## Startup Opportunities

**1. AI-Native MGU Platform (Gradient AI model)**

* Why this follows: M1 (market expansion) + M2 (consolidation) create demand for small group underwriting
* Wedge: Serve <500 life groups without claims history via data substitution
* Risk: Large carriers build in-house; Gradient AI already dominant; limited TAM ceiling if carriers internalize

**2. Gene Therapy-Specific Stop-Loss (BCS model)**

* Why this follows: M3 (structural pricing gap) creates new product category
* Wedge: Carve out gene therapy risk ($2-4.5M per treatment) from traditional stop-loss
* Risk: Frequency increases faster than pricing; adverse selection concentrates risk

**3. Level-Funded Product Infrastructure (WriteWise model)**

* Why this follows: M1 (SMB market unlock) requires Rx-specific solutions
* Wedge: Rx-only stop-loss with PMPM guarantees for 500-5K employees
* Risk: PBM integration complexity; carrier capacity constraints; competition from larger players
