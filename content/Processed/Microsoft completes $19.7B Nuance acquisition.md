# Microsoft Completes $19.7B Nuance Acquisition

Microsoft finalized its acquisition of Nuance Communications, an all-cash transaction of $19.7 billion including company debt ($56/share).

## Transaction Details

- **Announced:** April 2021
- **Completed:** March 2022
- **Price:** $19.7B ($56/share)
- **Second-largest MSFT deal** behind LinkedIn ($26.2B)

## Dragon Ambient eXperience (DAX)

Nuance's flagship healthcare product records doctor-patient meetings, creating clinical documentation that integrates with electronic health records.

- **Launched:** 2020
- **Function:** Converts conversations naturally into structured notes without dictation
- **Goal:** Combat clinician/radiologist burnout by automating documentation

## Strategic Rationale

Microsoft estimated acquisition would double total addressable market in healthcare to "nearly $500 billion" through Nuance's ambient clinical intelligence capabilities.

## Integration

- Mark Benjamin remained Nuance CEO, reporting to Scott Guthrie (EVP, Cloud + AI)
- Results reported as part of Microsoft's Intelligent Cloud division
- 550+ health systems using DAX

## Regulatory

- UK CMA: No competition concerns
- EU: Approved December 2021
- US: Approved June 2021
