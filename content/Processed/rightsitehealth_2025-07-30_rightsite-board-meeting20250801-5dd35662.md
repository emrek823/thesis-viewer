## Slide 3: Good Stuff and Stuff

**Good Stuff and Stuff**

### Good Stuff

Our focus for the remainder of 2025 is on achieving positive unit economics. This depends on three drivers: closing case rate contracts, scaling EMS call volume, and continuing to reduce the Care Team cost of care. We've made measurable progress on each front:

**Case Rate Contracts**
Several key payers, including United, Aetna, Cigna, and BCBS-TX, have made significant progress in the sales cycle. We attribute this progress to: (1) shifting to unit-based analysis rather than total dollar savings, which reduces the lift for MEU and actuarial teams; (2) using our growing EMS pipeline as leverage, making clear that without contracts we will need to send those members to the ER; (3) refocusing payer contracting conversations from national to state-level teams who we've seen move more quickly; and (4) engaging Havarti, our actuarial consultant, to strengthen our financial case including third-party validation.

**EMS Call Volume**
Our EMS pipeline has expanded rapidly in Texas, Georgia, and Florida. RightSite's reputation as a trusted partner is accelerating agency adoption. A growing number of EMS deals are coming from our competitor Tele911.

This growing call volume growth is not only ahead of forecast, but it is proving effective in creating credibility and urgency in payer contracting discussions.

### Good Stuff (cont.)

**Care Team Cost**
We're seeing encouraging trends in reducing per-episode costs. Targeted process refinements from time-and-motion studies, staffing schedule strategies with physicians, and more selective use of resources like rideshare, urgent care and prescription assistance, have lowered costs. We are more carefully allocating resources to patients covered by contracts while limiting spend on patients not covered by contracts. We expect Q3 to demonstrate further improvements in cost, and have set a long-term goal: can we ultimately deliver a full episode of care at or below our FFS reimbursement rate?

## Slide 4: Good Stuff and Stuff

**Good Stuff and Stuff**

### Stuff

**Payer Contracting Delays**
Despite the payer pipeline progress, we still need signed case rate contracts.

**EMS Account Management**
EMS sales and activation are currently outpacing our ability to staff and support the field. We are actively recruiting additional account manager coverage, but we are operating below what is needed to support existing demand.

**Care Team Cost**
The more we solve for Care Team cost reduction, the more opportunities we see to reduce it further. We have work to do here.

Our physician licensing and credentialing process is still inefficient, delaying collections (we have to retro-bill), and complicating physician scheduling as we work to align physician coverage with payer eligibility/credentialing.

**Engineering**
To maintain platform stability, we have deferred or slowed several feature releases, including analytics dashboards and navigator workflow tools. While this preserves core functionality, it limits our ability to drive additional cost-per-episode reductions and creates risk of operational drag in future quarters. Our first new engineer in two years joins the current team of three next week.

## Slide 18: Care Team Operations

**Care Team Operations**

### What Happened

The Care Team has two jobs: (1) deliver on our Brand Promise of being a trusted partner for Patients, EMS, and Plans, and (2) relentlessly lower the cost of providing high-quality care. Here's how we did:

**Operational Metrics**
*   **Staffing & Productivity:** Physicians now average 4.0 activations/hour, up from 2.0, while maintaining service standards. Call volume surges across most shifts are stretching current capacity.
*   **QA & Training:** Navigator QA reviews at 10% of all calls and physician QA at 5%, with bi-weekly coaching and feedback. New navigators receive frequent individual coaching, and existing staff receive monthly coaching. Weekly trainings reinforce process standardization and Motivational Interviewing skills.
*   **Employee Engagement:** New shift staffing schedules for Navigator, and a redesigned Care Team office space are strengthening culture and collaboration.

**Lowering Care Cost**
