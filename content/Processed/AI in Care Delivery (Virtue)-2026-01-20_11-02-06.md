### Healthcare AI Investment Landscape

- AI application layer facing commoditization pressure
	- Snowflake’s Cortex product allowing Series B+ companies to DIY application layer functionality
	- Scribes, voice prior auth identified as easily commoditized areas
	- Market shifting from hype to real execution focus
- Care delivery sentiment potentially improving among investors
	- Growing trepidation around software investments driving interest back to care delivery models
	- AI-enabled specialty care models gaining attention
- Portfolio activity highlights
	- Right Way (specialty pharmacy MGU) seeing strong market pull with 2-year PMPM guarantees
	- Autonomy (neurology) focused on specialty pharmacy value chain navigation
	- Q4 markups across portfolio, particularly in specialty pharmacy

### Market Dynamics & Product Developments

- EMR companies launching competitive AI products
	- Athena releasing free ambient scribe to compete with Nuance/DAX
	- Athena launching CDI competitor to Navina, releasing HCC by HCC
	- Toggle feature for “AI native encounter” changing entire UX
- ROI data becoming more prominent in vendor pitches
	- FTE replacement metrics, profit improvements, turnaround time gains
	- Pre Doc showing detailed ROI slides across multiple use cases
	- AI scribe generating ~$3K additional annual Medicare revenue per provider
- HIE intelligence layers trending
	- Multiple companies (Zeus, Pre Doc) building intelligence on top of HIEs
	- Provider-payer integration attempts bringing payer rules to point of care

### Portfolio Company Connections & Market Trends

- Hermes Health building neutral middleware for non-treatment use cases
	- Connects ROI/claims/imaging vendors under patient consent model
	- Expanding from legal into payers, providers, life sciences
- Specialty focus areas showing strength
	- Right Way’s employer specialty pharmacy carve-outs gaining traction
	- Mandolin, Tandem driving pharma monetization on provider side
- Emerging arbitrage opportunities
	- Sparks-type models using data to exploit imprecise underwriting
	- Easier in pharmacy vs medical due to unit cost predictability
