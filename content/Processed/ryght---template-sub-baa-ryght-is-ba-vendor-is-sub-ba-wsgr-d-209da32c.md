HIPAA Subcontractor Business Associate Agreement

This Subcontractor Business Associate Agreement (“Sub-BAA”) is entered into by and between Ryght, Inc. (“Business Associate”) and [Vendor Name] (“Subcontractor”) and is effective as of the effective date of the Services Agreement (“Effective Date”).  Business Associate and Subcontractor may be referred to herein collectively as the “Parties” or individually as a “Party.”  This Sub-BAA is incorporated into and made part of the Services Agreement.

WHEREAS, Subcontractor provides certain Services to Business Associate pursuant to the Services Agreement;

WHEREAS, in connection with the Services, Subcontractor creates, receives, maintains and/or transmits PHI from, to, or on behalf of Business Associate or Business Associate’s Covered Entity Customers, which information is subject to certain protections under the HIPAA Rules;

WHEREAS, Business Associate is a “business associate” of its Covered Entity Customers, and has certain responsibilities with respect to the PHI of its Covered Entity Customers;

WHEREAS, this Sub-BAA defines the rights and responsibilities of each Party with respect to PHI Used and/or Disclosed pursuant to this Sub-BAA and the Services Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, for good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

# Definitions.

## All terms used but not defined herein shall have the meaning set forth in the HIPAA Rules or the Services Agreement, as applicable; provided, however, that in the event of a conflict between defined terms, the HIPAA Rules shall control.

## The following terms are specifically defined as follows:

### “Business Associate” has the same meaning as the term “business associate” at 45 CFR 160.103, and in reference to the Party to this BAA, shall mean Ryght, Inc.

### “Covered Entity” has the same meaning as the term “covered entity” at 45 CFR 160.103.

### “Customer” means any current or future Covered Entity customer of Business Associate for or on behalf of whom Business Associate creates, receives, transmits, or maintains PHI.

### “Electronic Protected Health Information” or “ePHI” has the same general meaning as the term “electronic protected health information” at 45 C.F.R. § 160.103, but for purpose of this Sub-BAA is limited to the ePHI created, received, transmitted, or maintained by Subcontractor for or on behalf of Business Associate or its Customers.

### “HIPAA Rules” means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act, and the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, each as amended from time to time.

### “Protected Health Information” or “PHI” has the same generally meaning as the term “protected health information” at 45 C.F.R. § 160.103, but for purposes of this Sub-BAA is limited to the PHI created, received, transmitted, or maintained by Subcontractor for or on behalf of Business Associate or its Customers.

### “Services Agreement” means the [Name of Underlying Services Agreement] between the Parties effective [Effective Date of Underlying Agreement].

### “Services” means the services that Subcontractor provides to Business Associate pursuant to the Services Agreement.

### “Subcontractor” has the same meaning as the term “subcontractor” at 45 CFR 160.103, and in reference to the Party to this BAA, means [Vendor Name].

### “Subcontractor Vendor” means a “subcontractor” (as that term is defined at 45 CFR 160.103) to which Subcontractor subcontracts any Services as permitted under this Sub-BAA and/or the Services Agreement.

# Obligations and Activities of Subcontractor.

## Subcontractor agrees not to Use or Disclose PHI received or created by Subcontractor except as permitted by this Sub-BAA, the Services Agreement, or as Required by Law.

## To the extent the Subcontractor will carry out one or more of Business Associate’s or Business Associate’s Customers’ obligations under the Privacy Rule, Subcontractor shall comply with the requirements of the Privacy Rule that apply to the Business Associate and/or its Customers in the performance of such obligations.

## Subcontractor agrees to use appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards, and to comply with Subpart C of 45 CFR Part 164 with respect to Electronic PHI, to prevent Use or Disclosure of the information other than as provided for by this Sub-BAA.

## Subcontractor agrees to report promptly to Business Associate, and in any case within five (5) days, any Use or Disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Sub-BAA of which Subcontractor becomes aware, including a Breach of Unsecured PHI as required under 45 C.F.R. §164.410 and any Security Incident.  Subcontractor’s report shall include, to the extent possible, the identification of each Individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed by Subcontractor to have been, accessed, acquired, Used, or Disclosed during or as a result of the Breach. Subcontractor shall also provide Business Associate with at least the following information: (i) a description of the Breach, including the date of Breach and the date of discovery of the Breach, if known; (ii) a description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved in the Breach; (iii) any steps Individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm resulting from the Breach; (iv) a brief description of what Subcontractor is doing to investigate the Breach, to mitigate harm to Individuals, and to protect against any further Breaches; and (v) any other information requested by Business Associate or its Customers related to the Breach. Subcontractor shall supplement such notice with additional information as it becomes available, even if such information becomes available after Individuals have been notified of the Breach. Subcontractor shall reimburse Business Associate for all costs and expenses incurred by Business Associate and its Customers arising from a Breach by Subcontractor, including: all costs and expenses related to notifying Individuals, the Secretary, and the media of the Breach; any forensic investigations Business Associate or its Customers undertake related to the Breach; and all attorneys’ fees of Business Associate and its Customers related to the Breach.

## Subcontractor agrees to mitigate any harmful effect that is known to Subcontractor of a Use or Disclosure of PHI by Subcontractor or its employees, officers, Subcontractor Vendors, or agents that is not permitted under this Sub-BAA (including, without limitation, any Security Incident or Breach).  Subcontractor shall keep Business Associate fully apprised of all mitigation efforts of the Subcontractor required under this Section 2(e).

## Subcontractor shall ensure that any Subcontractor Vendor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate or its Customers as permitted under and pursuant to this Sub-BAA agrees in writing to the same terms and conditions that apply to Subcontractor under this Sub-BAA.

## Subcontractor agrees to make available, within ten (10) days of a request by Business Associate, PHI that is maintained in a Designated Record Set (if any), to Business Associate and/or its Customers, in each case as may be directed by Business Associate, in order for Business Associate and/or its Customers to satisfy their requirements under 45 C.F.R. §164.524.

## Subcontractor agrees to make any amendment(s) to PHI maintained in a Designated Record Set (if any), within ten days of a request by Business Associate, in order to enable Business Associate and/or its Customers to satisfy their obligations under 45 C.F.R. §164.526.

## Subcontractor agrees to maintain and make available to Business Associate and/or its Customers, within ten (10) days of a request by Business Associate, the information required for Business Associate and/or its Covered Entities to provide an accounting of Disclosures as necessary to satisfy Business Associate’s and/or its Customers’ obligations under 45 C.F.R. §164.528.

## For clarity, if an Individual makes any request for access to PHI related to the forgoing Sections 2(g)-(i), Subcontractor shall promptly forward such request to Business Associate within five (5) business days of the request and shall cooperate with Business Associate and/or its Customers in matters relating to such request; provided, however, that in no case shall Subcontractor reply directly to any Individual regarding such request.

## Subcontractor agrees to make its internal practices, books, and records, regarding the Use and Disclosure of PHI created or received by the Subcontractor on behalf of Business Associate and/or its Customers available to Business Associate or the Secretary for purposes of determining compliance with the HIPAA Rules.

# Permitted Uses and Disclosures by Subcontractor.

## Subcontractor may only Use or Disclose PHI as permitted in this Sub-BAA, the Services Agreement, or as Required by Law.

## Subcontractor may not Use or Disclose PHI in a manner that would violate Subpart E of 45 C.F.R. Part 164 if done by Business Associate or its Customers.

## Subcontractor may Use PHI for its proper management and administration, or to carry out its legal responsibilities

## Subcontractor may Disclose PHI for its proper management and administration, or to carry out the legal responsibilities, provided the Disclosures are (i) Required by Law, or (ii) Subcontractor obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the information is Disclosed that the information will remain confidential and Used or further Disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purposes for which it was Disclosed to the person, and the person notifies Subcontractor of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of the information has been breached.

## Any permitted Use or Disclosure under Sections 3(a)-(d) shall be consistent with the minimum necessary requirements set forth in the HIPAA Rules.

# Term and Termination.

## Term.  The Term of this Sub-BAA shall commence as of the Effective Date and, except for the rights and obligations set forth in this Sub-BAA specifically surviving termination, shall terminate upon the termination or expiration of the Services Agreement, unless otherwise earlier terminated for cause in accordance with this Section 4.

## Termination by Business Associate.  In addition to any termination provisions set forth in the applicable Services Agreement, Business Associate may terminate this Sub-BAA if Business Associate determines that Subcontractor has violated a material term of this Sub-BAA and Subcontractor has failed to cure the violation within thirty (30) days of notice by Business Associate to Subcontractor of such alleged violation.

## Effect of Termination.  At any time during the term of the Sub-BAA, and upon termination or expiration of this Sub-BAA for any reason, Subcontractor shall return to Business Associate or destroy (at Business Associate’s direction) any PHI that Subcontractor maintains in any form. Subcontractor shall direct any Subcontractor Vendors to return or destroy PHI in accordance with Business Associate’s direction under this Subsection 4(c).

# Indemnification.

## Subcontractor agrees to indemnify, defend and hold harmless Business Associate and its Customers, and each of its or their respective employees, directors/trustees, members, professional staff, representatives and agents (collectively, the “Indemnitees”) from and against any and all losses, fines, penalties, damages, expenses and costs (including attorney’s fees) arising out of any claims, actions, lawsuits or investigations in each case brought by a third party and which arise out of, result from or are connected to a breach of the terms and conditions of this Sub-BAA or a violation of the HIPAA Rules by Subcontractor or its employees or agents (including any Subcontractor Vendors).

## To the extent Subcontractor has limited its liability under the terms of the Services Agreement, whether with a maximum recovery for direct damages or a disclaimer against any consequential, indirect, or punitive damages, or other such limitations, such limitation shall not apply to the following:

### any indemnification obligation of Subcontractor under Section 5(a);

### the costs of notifying Individuals of a Breach involving their PHI as required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.400 et seq.;

### any civil monetary penalties, fines, or other damages resulting from the action of any state or federal government agency as a result of a Breach;

### fees of counsel, forensic computer specialists, and other consultants used to assist Business Associate or its Customers in responding to a Breach or any Security Incident and any subsequent investigation by a federal or state government agency; and

### the defense of lawsuits brought by Individuals alleging invasions of privacy, and any liability resulting from such lawsuits (whether in the form of a judgment or settlement), provided that Business Associate and its Customers shall have the opportunity to participate in the defense of such lawsuits and to approve any proposed settlement for which it would be financially responsible.

## This Section 5 shall survive termination or expiration of this Sub-BAA and Services Agreement for any reason.

# Miscellaneous.

This Sub-BAA is governed by, and will be construed in accordance with, the laws of the State that govern the Services Agreement. This Sub-BAA may only be assigned in connection with an assignment of the Services Agreement; any other assignment shall be null and void. If any part of a provision of this Sub-BAA is found illegal or unenforceable, it will be enforced to the maximum extent permissible, and the legality and enforceability of the remainder of that provision and all other provisions of this Sub-BAA will not be affected. All notices relating to the Parties’ legal rights and remedies under this Sub-BAA will be provided in writing to a Party, will be sent to its address set forth in the Services Agreement, or to such other address as may be designated by that Party by notice to the sending Party, and will reference this Sub-BAA. This Sub-BAA may be modified, or any rights under it waived, only by a written agreement executed by the authorized representatives of the Parties. The Parties agree to take such action as is necessary to amend this Sub-BAA from time to time as is necessary for the Parties to comply with the requirements of applicable law. A reference in this Sub-BAA to the HIPAA Rules means the provision or regulation as amended from time to time. This Sub-BAA is the complete and exclusive agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof, superseding and replacing all prior agreements, communications, and understandings (written and oral) regarding its subject matter. Any ambiguity in this Sub-BAA shall be resolved in favor of the meaning that permits the Parties to comply with applicable law and any current regulations promulgated thereunder. Any failure of a Party to exercise or enforce any of its rights under this Sub-BAA will not act as a waiver of such rights.
