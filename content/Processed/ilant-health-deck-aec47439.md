## Slide 2: The Obesity Condition

### The Obesity Condition

**Pervasive**
50%
of the US adult population will have obesity/severe obesity by 2030, up from 30% in 2000

**Costly**
$1.7T
spent to treat chronic conditions attributed to obesity with individuals with obesity cost 2-3x those that are at normal weight

**Unequal**
20%
greater impact on disadvantaged communities (black/brown, rural, lower income, lower education)

**Undertreated**
<1%
of the people who qualify medically for obesity treatment are in evidence-based care (medication, bariatric surgery)

## Slide 3: Why Now?

### Why Now?

**Growing Awareness**
* Obesity recognized as disease by AMA in 2013
* Covid increased awareness of obesity as independent risk factor
* Growing popular coverage on failure of diets

**Improved Treatments**
* Significant increase in bariatric surgery safety with laparoscopic care and sleeve option
* October 2022 change to extend recommended usage of bariatric surgery to 30 BMI with comorbidities, 35 without (4x increase in relevant population)
* Introduction of effective medication with multiple new approvals in last 3-5 years and additional drugs in clinical trial

**Regulatory & Payer Tailwinds**
* Wide-spread coverage of bariatric surgery (including in Medicaid)
* Increasing coverage of anti-obesity medications: required under federal employee plan in 2023
* Discussion around Treat and Reduce Obesity Act (TROA), which would require coverage

## Slide 4: The Problem...and Solution

### The Problem...and Solution

Obesity Challenges Similar to mental health and fertility 10 years ago

**Limited Understanding**
Only ~5000 obesity MDs while PCP lack time, knowledge, and are uncomfortable treating

**Marginalized Population**
Stigma, shame, and self-blame leads to poor engagement of lack of trusted clinical support

**Poor Access**
Payor ROI concerns result in limited coverage and complex prior authorization
Limited obesity medicine capacity drives 3-6 month waits

**Fragmented Solutions**
Existing players "Point solutions" (e.g., bariatric only, direct-to-consumer-GLP-1s) and new competitors are targeting lower efficacy solutions

Ilant addresses these challenging – destigmatizing care and increasing access

**Single Front Door**
Single point of access for all evidence-based solutions (intense behavioral therapy, medication, bariatric surgery), removing fragmentation and enabling treatment progression

**ROI-Driven**
Focus on higher cost, higher acuity populations where treatment deliver the highest ROI – and the greatest immediate impact on health

**Technology Enablement**
Use technology to enable more personalized and tailored care while increasing clinical efficiency – providing greater care access

**Behavioral Economics**
Combat common barriers to seeking/engaging with care using tools from behavioral economics: peer coaches, self-set goals

## Slide 12: How we Deliver ROI

### How we Deliver ROI

*   Focus on **repeated cost** (drugs, equipment) associated with obesity-related, reversible conditions
*   Focus on individuals with **higher BMI**, which is associated with higher cost (diabetic with 45+ BMI 2x more costly than diabetic with 40-45 BMI)
*   Optimize **site-of-care** (bariatric surgery is <$10K outpatient and $25K+ inpatient)
*   Reduce **unnecessary pre-surgery requirements** and **post-surgery ER/urgent care** use

**ROI Example**

| Item                                    | Value       |
| :-------------------------------------- | :---------- |
| Net Annual Savings (Drug/DME Savings less Ilant Cost) | $10-20K     |
| One-time Cost Bariatric Surgery         | $10-25K     |
| **2-year ROI**                          | **1 to 3x** |

**Additional expected sources of value:**
*   ER/Inpatient stays
*   Other chronic conditions (e.g., CKD, MSK)
*   Population health improvements (e.g., cancer, stroke)
*   Member retention/longevity
*   Employee productivity
*   Disability & workers comp claims
