The move aligns with the Trump administration’s government efficiency goals.

The Food and Drug Administration is working to centralize adverse events reporting, according to two current FDA staff granted anonymity to discuss the moves.

The new system would combine vaccine, drug, and device adverse event reporting systems into one automated platform, according to the two employees. It would also use artificial intelligence to identify potential or common issues with FDA authorized and approved products early, the people said. The aim would be to find adverse events in real-time. They added that they were not provided details on how these changes would be achieved.

Right now, the FDA has two ways of tracking problems with devices, biologics, and drugs. One requires patients, doctors, and device makers to report adverse events. The other, known as the Sentinel Initiative, allows the agency to query a network of health systems, payers, and other health data holders about the safety of a given product. It is not clear if Sentinel would be combined with the FDA Adverse Event Reporting System, these people said.
