## Summary
We're building Callie and our ambition is to create the dominant consumer healthcare company for the next decade and beyond.

Introducing Callie, an adorable AI that will become the face of healthcare.

Callie is a modern virtual behavioral health hospital powered by multimodal LLMs. Most of our patients are 18-25 year olds with co-occurring conditions such as depression, anxiety, eating disorders, OCD, and BPD.

## Slide 3
Our product fundamentally redefines the modern behavioral health experience from the ground-up through two layers.

### A. LLM Front Door & Admissions
Provide an LLM medical assistant (a cute cat!) who patients build a relationship with and talk about their behavioral health issues. The medical assistant primarily does two things:
1.  Administers psychotherapy/medical nutritional therapy through text and voice.
2.  Acts as a care coordinator: routes patients to the right care based on their coverage benefits and their conditions.

### B. Clinical Services
Provide a layer of clinical services administered by human clinicians. We work with clinicians in two ways:
1.  Business in a box: Empower clinicians to start their own practices under our verticalized MSO/PC. I helped write an article regarding this model.

## Monetization
We discuss both short-term and long-term monetization opportunities below.
1.  Consumer Saas. Through a freemium model, charge patients a recurring subscription and usage-based transactional fees (like Headspace, Calm but better).
2.  Kickback Partnerships: Send benefits-verified patients who are high acuity or require specialized treatment to care delivery partners. Charge Saas fee by patient volume - currently in discussions with a care delivery partner (paying 4 figures per qualified patient).
3.  Business In A Box (BIAB): Take rate on FFS insurance reimbursement through our verticalized MSO/PC with 1099 clinicians.

## Why now?
*   BIAB businesses in behavioral health grow insanely fast. Grow Therapy, Headway, Charlie Health, and others have grown to 9 figures in ARR in 3 years or fewer. We sit on the very monetization motions that these businesses have been built on while creating a stronger relationship with patients in retention and brand.
*   Exponential improvements in AI infrastructure. Models will outperform Master's level & licensed clinicians (psychotherapists, dietitians) within the next 18 months in terms of patient outcomes. They will offer better memory, personalization, knowledge in cutting-edge research, instant 24/7 access, flexible session lengths, and more. Leveraging this, we can demonstrate the best patient outcomes (ie. PHQ-9, GAD-7) in behavioral health.

Our product also automatically improves on its own as technology continues to develop at the infrastructure and foundational level.

*   "Hair on fire" problem. Market is massively underserved and patients are screaming for a solution. CPM ($1.5 CPA and ~$10-15 CPFTS) is the cheapest we've seen across 100s of consumer products.
*   Preexisting stigma being normalized. Like how jogging, working out, meditation, and therapy were once stigmatized and then normalized, talking to an AI for behavioral health issues is becoming mainstream.

Destigmatization creates massive consumer waves resulting in businesses like Nike, Calm, and Talkspace being built. The next wave is just starting now and Callie will ride it all the way.

*   Explosion of pet-based apps. Finch was bootstrapped to 10 million users in 3 years, Pokemon Sleep dominates the top charts, and Sush organically hit millions of users. There's a huge gap for a serious player in the healthcare category akin to when social networks were just Friendster and MySpace.
*   Consumer engagement for LLMs is unprecedented. Patient engagement is one of the largest unsolved issues in care delivery. Bring Callie's engagement to healthcare rails to solve one of the most lucrative problems in healthcare.

## Competitors
Market is highly fragmented because it's one of the largest markets in consumer ($80B+).
1.  Tech-enabled outpatient incumbents (e.g. Betterhelp, Two Chairs) - funded by mix of venture & private equity. Struggles with provider churn and brand does not resonate with Gen Z.
2.  Private practice therapists & dietitians: some completely cash pay while others do mix. This slice of the market is small but always will exist.
3.  New Marketplaces (e.g. Headway, Nourish): generally good businesses but provider quality decreases at scale. They can't enter AI space b/c of Innovator's Dilemma.
4.  AI companions (e.g. Character.ai): have a different audience (mostly male) and are generally not healthcare-focused enough to monetize well.
5.  Meditation apps (e.g. Calm, Headspace): some products are $100M+ ARR but most of Gen Z does not have the attention span to meditate.
6.  Virtual pet apps (e.g. Finch, Talking Tom): digital pets have appealed to consumer psychology. Existing ones aren't focused on healthcare.
