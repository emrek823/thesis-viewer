## Slide 3: Healthcare has a liability problem
# Healthcare has a liability problem

A 2023 AMA research paper found that **31% of all physicians had been sued at some point in their careers.**¹

US medical liability costs estimated at **$55.6 billion annually in 2010.²**

78% of claims did not result in an indemnity payment.³

## Slide 4: AI is compounding the liability problem
# AI is compounding the liability problem

The integration of AI into healthcare practices presents more liabilities than usual:
*   "Black Box" decision making
*   Ethical dilemmas
*   Data privacy issues
*   Regulatory compliance
*   Biases in AI algorithms
*   Errors in AI-driven decision-making

These risks pose significant barriers to the safe and effective deployment of AI technologies.

Understanding the reasons or causes that explain why an AI system x decided that y is the right decision or course of action is generally not possible.⁴

And the number of AI products coming to market will **five-fold** in the next few years.⁵

## Slide 5: AI technology is viewed as a product, not a person (for now), however, the surface area of liabilities is rapidly increasing:
AI technology is viewed as a product, not a person (for now), however, the surface area of liabilities is rapidly increasing:

**Medical Malpractice:** If the healthcare provider's actions or failure to act fell below the accepted standard of care, resulting in harm to you, you may have grounds for a medical malpractice lawsuit. This could include cases where the provider negligently relied on or misinterpreted AI recommendations.

**Product Liability:** If the AI system itself was defective or malfunctioned, leading to your injury, you might have a product liability claim against the manufacturer or distributor of the AI system.

**Breach of Contract:** If you had a contractual agreement with the healthcare provider, and they failed to provide the agreed-upon standard of care, you could file a breach of contract lawsuit.

**Negligence:** In addition to medical malpractice, you may have a negligence claim if the healthcare provider failed to exercise reasonable care in their use of the AI recommendation, resulting in harm to you.

**Informed Consent:** If the healthcare provider failed to adequately inform you about the risks associated with using AI recommendations in your treatment, and you suffered harm as a result, you might have a claim for lack of informed consent.

**Fraud or Misrepresentation:** If the healthcare provider made false or misleading statements about the capabilities or reliability of the AI system, and you relied on those statements to your detriment, you could potentially bring a lawsuit for fraud or misrepresentation.

## Slide 11: Product Overview
# Product Overview

The Magnesium platform employs a modular architecture that integrates directly with healthcare AI systems via APIs, facilitating data exchange while upholding privacy standards. It utilizes machine learning algorithms to analyze AI decisions, spotting deviations from expected outcomes or ethical norms. Continuous scanning for regulatory compliance (HIPAA/GDPR) is achieved through automated checks against a regularly updated rules database. Alerts and dashboards provide real-time insights into compliance status, ethical considerations, bias, and risk levels, enabling healthcare organizations to make informed decisions and maintain transparency in AI-driven processes.

1)  **Sign Up:** Register your healthcare organization on our platform.
2)  **API Integration:** Connect your AI systems with our platform using provided APIs.
3)  **Configuration:** Set up monitoring preferences and compliance requirements.
4)  **Automated Scanning:** The platform begins continuous analysis of AI decisions and data handling.
5)  **Alerts Setup:** Configure real-time alerts for compliance deviations or ethical issues.
6)  **Dashboard Monitoring:** Use the dashboard to view compliance status, risk assessments, audit logs and actionable insights. Produces on demand reports.
7)  **Review and Adjust:** Based on insights, make necessary adjustments to AI systems to enhance compliance and ethics.
8)  **Continuous Improvement:** Leverage platform feedback for ongoing enhancement of AI operations.
