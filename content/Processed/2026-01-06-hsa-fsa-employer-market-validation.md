# HSA/FSA Employer Market Is Real But Consolidating—Window for Pure-Play HSA Startups Closing

**Lead take:** The HSA/FSA market is definitively real—$159B in assets, 40M accounts, 16% YoY growth, and major legislative tailwinds (7.3M newly HSA-eligible via One Big Beautiful Bill). But the startup opportunity is narrowing. HealthEquity has acquired its way to dominance ($2.5B+ in M&A), funded startups like Lively ($42M raised) and Elevate ($43M) are competing on margin, not product innovation, and the real action has shifted to **ICHRA platforms** (Thatch, Take Command) where regulatory arbitrage creates a genuine wedge. Pure-play HSA fintech is increasingly consulting vaporware; ICHRA-first benefits administration is the investable thesis.

**Confidence:** HIGH — Multiple independent sources confirm market size. Vault contains Thatch/BESPOKE/StretchDollar diligence memos. Legislative changes validated via IRS guidance.

---

## The Market Is Real: $159B and Growing

### HSA Assets Compound at 16% YoY

Per [Devenir Midyear 2025](https://www.devenir.com/hsa-assets-surge-to-159-billion-as-invested-assets-jump-30-year-over-year-to-reach-73-billion/):
- **$159B** total HSA assets across **40M accounts** (midyear 2025)
- 16% YoY asset growth, 6% account growth
- Investment assets: $73B (+30% YoY), now 46% of total
- Projected: **$208B by 2027** across 47M accounts

The growth is real and accelerating, driven by HDHP adoption and increasing recognition of HSAs as retirement vehicles.

### Legislative Tailwinds Are Massive

The **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** (July 2025) created the largest HSA eligibility expansion since HSAs were created:

1. **Bronze/Catastrophic plans now HSA-compatible** (effective Jan 2026): ~7.3M Marketplace enrollees newly eligible for HSAs without changing plans ([White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/research/2025/09/expansion-of-hsa-eligibility-under-obbb-act-to-improve-marketplace-coverage-affordability-and-access/))
2. **Direct primary care + HSA now allowed**: DPC memberships up to $150/month don't disqualify HSA eligibility
3. **Permanent telehealth first-dollar coverage**: HDHPs can cover telehealth pre-deductible

Additionally, **ACA enhanced subsidies expire Jan 2026**, pushing 22M enrollees toward HSA/ICHRA alternatives [[times-up-enhanced-aca-subsidies-expire]].

### TAM for Platforms: $28B-$146B

From vault [[gc-thatch-memo-2-3130da7e]]:
- **SMB/startup market (10-50 employees):** $28B (24M lives)
- **SMB + mid-market (<1000):** $85B (73M lives)
- **Full ICHRA displacement of self-insured:** $146B (125M lives)

## The Problem Is Real: 67% User Dissatisfaction

### Employer Pain Points

From vault [[gc-thatch-memo-2-3130da7e]]:
> "Rising costs are driving uptake of high-deductible health plans and HSA utilization; yet **67% of HSA-users find them hard to use** and frustrating to manage."

Broker quote from Marsh McLennan:
> "Why are brokers looking for alternatives? Because your competition is bloated, out of touch, and complicated. If you can streamline this approach for us, you'll increase your reach."

### Specific Friction Points

Per [Lively](https://livelyme.com/blog/what-to-know-about-transferring-your-employees-hsas):
- High exit fees limit portability
- Paper-based enrollment delays access
- Bulk transfers often require individual employee action
- Integration with payroll/benefits admin is manual

## The Investment Thesis Has Shifted

### What's NOT Investable Anymore

**Pure-play HSA fintech** is largely played out:
- Zero-fee positioning commoditized (Lively, First Dollar, Starship all zero-fee)
- UX differentiation insufficient vs. incumbent scale
- HealthEquity can acquire any winner at 3-5x revenue
- No clear path to $100M+ ARR as standalone HSA

### What IS Investable

**1. ICHRA-first benefits administration**
- Regulatory arbitrage: ICHRA allows employers to fund individual coverage
- 3M → 11-12M lives by 2026 creates greenfield
- Thatch positioning as "ICHRA + HSA + marketplace" integrated stack
- 65M SMB employees at risk as carriers exit small group [[stretchdollar-f3b65d77]]

**2. AI-native benefits admin (broader category)**
- 32Health example: $6.4M ARR, 233% YoY growth, automating claims/credentialing [[32health-series-a-complete-deck]]
- Benefits admin is $1.6T TAM with massive manual workflow waste

**3. Distribution plays**
- Brokers control employer benefits decisions
- Broker-first GTM (Thatch model) vs. direct-to-SMB (StretchDollar) is key strategic choice

## Bull/Bear Cases

### Bull Case (ICHRA captures employer benefits)
- ICHRA reaches 15M+ lives by 2028
- ACA subsidy expiration forces mass migration to individual coverage + HRAs
- Thatch or similar builds broker network effect, becomes "Rippling for benefits"
- HealthEquity slow to adapt to ICHRA (focused on HSA custody)

### Bear Case (Incumbents adapt, startups stall)
- HealthEquity acquires leading ICHRA platform
- Broker inertia keeps employers on group plans
- Legislative tailwinds reversed (ACA subsidies extended)
- WEX/Alegeus add ICHRA capabilities, distribution advantage wins

---

## Recommendations

1. **Skip pure-play HSA investments** — Market is real but consolidating. HealthEquity will acquire winners.

2. **Source ICHRA-first platforms** — Thatch-like models with broker distribution have venture-scale potential. Look for: ICHRA + HSA + marketplace integration, broker network effects.

3. **Watch for AI-native benefits admin** — 32Health pattern: automate manual workflows (claims, credentialing, eligibility), sell to TPAs/payers. Adjacent to HSA but higher margin.

4. **Monitor HealthEquity M&A** — If they acquire Thatch or similar, confirms ICHRA thesis but caps startup outcomes.
