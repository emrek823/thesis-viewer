## Slide 3: Pharma Market is in Need of R&D Efficiency
Pharma Market is in Need of R&D Efficiency

R&D ROI has declined precipitously as looming patent expirations on blockbuster drug also threaten peak sales per pipeline

*   **$1 Trillion** size of the global pharmaceutical industry today, expected to reach **$2T by 2027**
*   **$2.5 Billion** estimated sponsor cost per new drug approved, a **145% increase** over the last 15 years
*   **5-7%** of first-in-human drugs gain FDA approval, a **1.8% ROI** and **~6x ROI decline** since 2010
*   **$1.5 Billion** per approved new drug is attributed to clinical development

## Slide 4: Modernizing Clinical Drug Development is a Large Opportunity
Modernizing Clinical Drug Development is a Large
Opportunity

The clinical trials / development process makes up nearly 70% of overall drug development costs

| | Drug Development (12-15 years) | Drug Launch and Sales (Ongoing) |
| :---------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| | **Research & Discovery** (1/3 of costs) | **Development** (2/3 of costs) | **Delivery & Sales** |
| **Description** | Identifying novel therapeutics to treat disease by researching the biology of cellular targets, screening chemical compounds to find compound-target fits, and making iterative chemical changes to maximize properties of interest and minimize toxicity | Research studies on disease states or potentially promising therapies for disease states that are often performed in human subjects (or using existing data on human subjects) in order to understand the efficacy of a treatment and identify potentially harmful side effects | Enabling pharmaceutical market access by establishing a product's clinical advantage, educating prescribers, securing formulary positioning and marketing the product |
| **Challenges** | *   New targets needed
*   Inherently "iterative" process of target discovery, due to our evolving understanding of biological systems
*   Size of compound libraries (billions/trillions) limits speed of evaluation
*   "Noisy" research data | *   Study design / adaptability
*   Site selection and accessibility (exacerbated by C19)
*   Patient recruitment and matching
*   Patient engagement
*   Endpoint tracking and data collection
*   Supply chain management | *   More than one-third of drugs failed to meet launch expectations
*   Limited market access, limited understanding of market needs and poor product differentiation are common contributing factors |
| **Opportunities** | *   In-silico manipulation of biological systems at scale to streamline modeling and inferences
*   AI/ML to better model and represent biological systems, predict pharmacodynamics & identify drug biomarkers | *   Currently, pharma outsources 75% of this activity, a $75B market
*   Deeper integration with data sources, broader data monitoring and a patient centric approach to trial design are significant opportunities | *   End-to-end strategic planning, based on a therapeutic area, portfolio strategy, innovative approaches to market intelligence and stakeholder engagement and better performance monitoring are significant opportunities |

## Slide 6: Development: Opportunities to Improve the Clinical Trial Workflow
Development: Opportunities to Improve the
Clinical Trial Workflow

Trials aim to increasingly become more patient-centric, data-driven, distributed and centrally managed in nature

*   **Protocol Design**
    *   Design patient-centric, data-driven, adaptive protocols that can be adjusted based on interim milestones
    *   Leverage big data and EMR connectivity to optimize endpoint development and study design
    *   This would reduce the need for trial protocol amendments and reduce development timelines
*   **Patient Recruitment**
    *   Deep EMR integration, rich patient datasets and a direct position within the day-to-day physician workflow can automate the ability to identify and optimize patient-trial fit
    *   Increasingly, D2C approaches are leveraging patient-reported data to power self-enrollment efforts
*   **Patient Engagement**
    *   Offering fully virtual or "hybrid" engagement models, sequencing home visits, virtual visits and physical visits to regularly and accessibly engage patients, as well as collect clinical data
    *   Creating incentives to streamline and optimize the patient experience & financially align interests
*   **Data Collection, Analytics & Insights**
    *   Remote patient endpoint monitoring is increasingly favored as a convenient clinical data collection mechanism that can also increase the amount of data collected & potentially enhance statistical effect size
    *   Real world evidence is also being used to simulate virtual control arms, reducing trial burden
    *   Directly flowing clinical outcome data into the EHR can also streamline data handling costs
*   **Operations & Supply Chain**
    *   Centralize and automate supply chain management by connecting sites, clinical operations and vendors with real-time patient data, allowing longitudinal visibility into supply chain and the ability to forecast orders and manage distribution

## Slide 7: Trial Enrollment Process
Trial Enrollment Process

### Clinical Trial Enrollment Flowchart

**EHR integration**
*   Clinical data (EHR/Lab/Rad)

**Triggering clinical event**
*   NGS results
*   Provider request
*   Disease state change
*   Treatment change
*   New diagnosis

These events feed into **Patient to trial match**.

**Patient to trial match** also receives input from **New trial patient match**.

**From Patient to trial match, the flow splits:**
1.  To **PI/study coordinator notification**
2.  To **Initial prescreening**

**PI/study coordinator notification** leads to **Provider notification** and **NGS reports**.

**Initial prescreening** leads to **Final prescreening**.

**Final prescreening** leads to **Consent and screening**.

**Consent and screening** leads to **On-study management**.

A path from **Final prescreening** also leads to **Waitlist management**.

### Clinical trial data model (supporting information)
This model, for eligibility criteria, includes:
*   Diagnosis
*   NGS biomarker eligibility criteria
*   Protein biomarkers
*   Cytogenetic markers
*   Eligibility checklist
*   Treatment context
*   Prior therapies
