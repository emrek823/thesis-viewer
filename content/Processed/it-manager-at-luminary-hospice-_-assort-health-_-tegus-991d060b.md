# Assort Health - IT Manager at Luminary Hospice

Interview conducted on July 23, 2025

## Topics
Healthcare, AI capabilities, Call Routing, AI Component, EHR Integration, Patient Intake, Customization, Voice AI

## Summary
The Tegus Client, researching voice AI in healthcare, spoke with an expert from a hospice service company with over 10 locations in four states. Initially using Parakeet for call triaging, they switched to Assort Health for its better AI capabilities and integration with their EHR system. Assort Health handles about 5,000 calls annually, costing over $150,000, with customization tailored to patient profiles. The expert highlighted the challenges of switching to a cheaper solution due to customization and integration difficulties, and discussed potential ways for Assort Health to generate additional ROI and become a core OS for non-clinical operations in the future.

## Expert Details
* IT Manager at Luminary Hospice. The expert can discuss being a key decision maker during the evaluation process of Assort Health.
* Current IT Manager at Luminary Hospice.
* Former Health Information Technology (HIT) Manager at Tennessee Primary Care Association, leaving in October 2024. Responsible for the 29 federally qualified health centers throughout the state of Tennessee, that are members of the primary care association. The expert conducted thorough assessments of each FQHC to identify needs related to health IT implementation and Meaningful Use, Electronic Health Record (EHR) platforms, Health Information Exchange (HIE), and telehealth. This includes providing education, coaching, and training in the evaluation, selection, implementation, and maintenance of information systems to ensure each health center optimizes its investment in operational HIT systems.
* Prior to the Tennessee Primary Care Association, the expert was the Clinical Systems Admin, EMR Specialist at Community Health Systems. The expert managed administrative processes, inventory, disaster recovery, research, outreach on new and emerging technologies, outsourced solutions, and operational monitoring of IT resources. The expert administered all onsite support for physicians and staff of the medical group as IT support and professional development for all technology resources related to computers, handheld devices, printers, phones, and clinical EMR (electronic medical records).
