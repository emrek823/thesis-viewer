### PPEC Market Overview

- Florida currently has most PPEC patients of any state; lowest reimbursement rate among 15 states with PPEC model
- 58 centers in Miami alone - highly saturated market
- Recent Medicaid audits revealed significant overpayments:
	- One Miami center required to repay $1.9M
	- Another Miami center required to repay $1.2M
- Every state has different licensing regulations and requirements
- Texas recently raised reimbursement to $398/day to address staffing issues and align with PDN rates
- New states planning to adopt PPEC model:
	- Virginia opening PPEC regulations in 2026
	- Missouri developing regulations
	- Ohio working on implementation

### Clinical Operations

- All Medicaid-based; no private insurance coverage
- Staffing ratios:
	- 3:1 patient ratio
	- Alternates between RN, CNA, LPN
	- Need 2 RNs in building for 12 kids
- Hours and billing:
	- Maximum 12 hours per day
	- First 4 hours billed hourly
	- 4 hours 15 minutes considered full day (different payment rate)
	- Most kids stay 6-8 hours
- Operating schedule:
	- Most centers open 6 days/week
	- Some patients attend Mon-Fri and Saturday
- Staffing challenges:
	- 96% retention rate pre-COVID
	- Significant drop post-COVID
	- Cannot compete with hospital pay rates
	- Particularly challenging in Florida due to low reimbursement
