## Slide 3: Background on quality registries

**What is a clinical quality registry?**
* Unique registry is completed for most surgeries, inpatient stays, and serious conditions
* Submitted to 3rd party association or society for quality benchmarking (e.g. ACC, AHA, STS, ACS)
* Data is used for quality control, benchmarking and is required to maintain accreditation
* Data abstraction is usually performed by clinically-trained staff (nurses)

**Challenges:**
* Hundreds of structured fields, but EHR data is unstructured
* Terminology and location in the record vary
* Human abstraction requires clinical and registry-specific experience
* Difficult to find and retain qualified staff
* AI-based abstraction wasn't possible at scale until now
