Maveron's name unites "maverick" and "vision." Samuel Augustus Maverick was a 19th-century pioneer in old Texas. After winning 300 head of cattle in a poker game, he defied prairie tradition and, out of humaneness, refused to brand his cattle. It was a bold move — and it took vision. The result: Every steer without a brand was called a "maverick" and the word entered the lexicon.  
  
**At Maveron, we prize the ability to look beyond convention and work with founders who are building the leading businesses of the future. Vision and people - not deals. It's branded into our name.**
