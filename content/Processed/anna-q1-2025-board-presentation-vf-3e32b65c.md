## Slide 3: CEO UPDATE - Highlights from Q1 2025

**Highlights from Q1 2025**

*   **0-to-1:** March 2025 ARR was $2.67 million, a 138x increase YoY from March 2024 ARR of $19,176. The traction from a standing start has proven the de novo model exceptionally well
*   **Quarterly Growth:** Q1 '25 ARR of $2.67 million is also a 2.67x increase from Q4 '24 of $1 million, reflecting continued strong growth in demand for ANNA's clinical model
*   **Capital Efficiency:** ANNA burned ~$74K in Mar-25, 57% less than Dec-24 while growing considerably
*   **Market Share:** An increasing proportion of ANNA's clients are coming from other ABA companies, demonstrating the efficacy of ANNA's clinical model and positioning
*   **Multi-Site Expansion:** Wayland launch was very effective—34 FDE clients at intake well above entire capacity, adding 6,000 square feet for excess capacity on < $50K investment
*   **AI Implementation:** Implemented AI in intakes, eligibility checks, benefit verification, prior authorizations, session note writing—all driving significant improvements to margin and scalability
*   **RCM:** 95% clean claims from RCM hire and AI implementation + Carelon A/R clean up

## Slide 4: CEO UPDATE - Challenges from Q1 2025

**Challenges from Q1 2025**

*   **Real Estate Timelines:** Lapse in timeline between when Sudbury was filled and when Wayland was operational led to loss of referrals.
    *   **Mitigant:** Open new locations while ramping previous ones
*   **Operational Discipline:** Timelines from intake to intervention were 3+ months, leading to loss of referrals and a slowdown in revenue realization. Need more operational infrastructure to playbook, measure, and intervene here and throughout the org.
    *   **Mitigant:** Hiring SVP of Operations
*   **BCBA Billing:** BCBA billing has improved to profitable but still has a ways to go to reach our targets.
    *   **Mitigant:** New bonus compensation plan, new review structure, and SVP of Ops hire
*   **Ancillary Services:** Rollout of new services remains slow, lack of capacity to prioritize
    *   **Mitigant:** Starting with social group (same staff)

## Slide 17: VENTURE DEBT - Venture Debt Overview

**Venture Debt Overview**

*   **4-Wall Math:** Total OpEx and CapEx to breakeven on a center is ~$300 – 400K. Annual cash flow at maturity is $1.5mm, reflecting a 3.75 – 5x+ multiple
*   **Breakeven:** ANNA centers breakeven at month 7 (P&L) and month 9 (cash flow)
*   **Venture Debt:** Lending at SOFR + 3% (~7.32%) allows us to dividend out the full investment amount back to redeploy upon reaching profitability, maintaining strong coverage.
*   **Moderate Leverage Returns:** Unlevered 5-year IRR is 375% with 15-months to payback, whereas levered 5-year IRR is 600% with 9-month payback ($300K * 7.32% = $21,960 of annual interest expense vs. $1.5mm in unlevered cash flow)
*   **High Leverage Returns:** Issuing 2x the dividend of the investment amount (thus enabling infinite 2x redeployment within 9-months), drives 5-year IRR to 979%. ($600K * 7.32% = $43,920 of annual interest expense vs. $1.5mm in unlevered cash flow)
