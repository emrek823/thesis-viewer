## Slide 2: Large Biological Models - Executive Summary
# Large Biological Models
### Executive Summary
LBM's mission is to be the quiet backbone of biomedical AI. Like LLM's, but for raw biomedical data. We make multimodal AI in the biomedicine as easy as LLM's make NLP: we provide multimodal foundation models trained on raw *omics, images, health record data to domain experts and data scientists to develop their own state-of-the-art models in small targeted domains.

Our Go-to-Market strategy is a Go-to-Data strategy, guided by three observations:
*   **1 | DOMAIN EXPERTS MUST OWN BIOMEDICAL AI APPLICATIONS**
*   **2 | DATA SCALE AND QUALITY DRIVE AI PERFORMANCE**
*   **3 | BIOMEDICAL DATA IS SILOED**

Our strategy optimizes AI performance by optimizing for quality data access. We then offer foundation model access that allows domain experts to build models as if they had access to the entire world's data.

Our approach begins with patient-level oncology data, fueled by a design partnership with a large biopharma. Long-term, we are not a life sciences company - we will fuel every biomedical AI application.

## Slide 3: Biomedical AI is too hard.
# Biomedical AI is too hard.
### The Problem

| Category                 | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :----------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **DATA IS SILOED**       | Biomedical data is isolated by site and modality. This is the most important barrier to building performant biomedical AI                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| **AI EXPERTISE IS TOO EXPENSIVE** | The first 90% of biomedical AI is the same. AI models universally take input and create a compressed representation. There only needs to be a handful of foundation models in biomedical AI.                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **DOMAIN EXPERTISE IS TOO IMPORTANT** | Domain expertise matters in clinician-facing applications. The last 10% does not scale. Edge cases differ, uses differ, and outcome measurements differ too wildly to be owned by one model or organization. These edge cases are time-consuming, do not replicate across tasks, and take 90% of the time and effort. |

## Slide 7: First Design Partnership: Large Biopharma
# First Design Partnership: Large Biopharma
### (Key details withheld from deck)

**WIDESPREAD DATA ACCESS TO THE RICHEST ONCOLOGY DATASET IN THE WORLD**
**$750K TARGET REVENUE IN YEAR 1**
**ON-PREMISES GPU COMPUTE**
**ABILITY TO EXPORT MODEL WEIGHTS**
**INITIAL FOCUS ON TRIAL RECRUITMENT**

**Overview:** Our first design partnership is with a large biopharma with an incredible presence in oncology. Our partnership would offer access to the entire real-world oncology data collection licensed by this biopharma, and a phased approach to access internal trial data, starting with NSCLC (the largest market in oncology). Following the pharma adage "collaborate on the tools, compete on the assets", we have explicitly reached an agreement to export and reuse the foundation model with other organizations.

**Deliverables:** In return, our design partner receives a foundation model tuned to patient recruitment by 1) identifying candidate patients from an existing feed and 2) predicting time-to-line-of-therapy-transfer. Patient recruitment is typically delayed 9-18mo, and on average (across all trials) trial delays cost >10M in revenue per month of delay.

**Land-and-expand:** Following the successful delivery of this model in July 2023, we will expand to other prognosis-related endeavors related to time-to-progression: line-of-therapy-transfer in commercial, digital twins, covariate adjustment, and predictive models of treatment efficacy. At each phase, we will take one further step back to empower our design partner to use the foundation model for downstream tasks rather than replicate internal expertise.

## Slide 8: First Design Partnership - Workflow
# First Design Partnership
### Workflow

Entire portfolio of oncology real-world data as well as clinical trial data from closed trials.

**Workflow Steps:**
1.  **ENTIRE PORTFOLIO OF ONCOLOGY RWD**
2.  **MULTIMODAL FOUNDATION MODEL**
    *   We create a multimodal foundation model from this body of data before building a model for a specific task. This lets us land and expand extremely quickly for other downstream tasks within the same organization: prognostic models in development, line-of-therapy transfer in commercial, etc.
3.  **FINE-TUNE FOR PATIENT RECRUITMENT**
    *   Our first deliverable is a model that identifies candidate patients for oncology trials and estimates the time at which they will become eligible. This leverages existing data feeds and does not require external infrastructure.

## Slide 9: Product roadmap - High level
# Product roadmap
### High level

Our mission is to accelerate biomedical AI by serving as its quiet backbone. Our ideal product is a foundation model served via API or fine-tunable model.

Downstream domain experts tune this model toward a plethora of similar but fundamentally unscalable tasks: clinical trial recruitment, line-of-therapy transfer prediction for commercial arms of biopharma, predictive models for treatment selection (and equivalently companion-diagnostics for clinical trial acceleration), digital twins via time-to-progression prognostic models, patient data validation for real-world-evidence companies, image segmentation models for surgical preparation, and more.

In 5 years, every biomedical AI team will achieve their best performance more quickly using our models, just like every NLP researcher uses an LLM. Otherwise, they will risk performance deficits, regulatory approvals, and revenue.

## Slide 10: Product roadmap - Focused level
# Product roadmap
### Focused level

Our first design partnerships deliberately target large biopharma. Pharma companies have the highest quality multimodal data, substantial amounts for pilots, have the world's best domain expertise in biomedicine.

We specifically target oncology because its data is fundamentally similar across organizations, it has a rapidly-evolving standard of care and complex treatment landscape (consider that there are cardiologists and gastroenterologists who exclusively manage adverse events from a handful of cancer therapies), and the biomedical AI space in oncology is most robust.

Our first design partnership is with a top biopharma with the world's richest oncology portfolio. Our first deliverable is a foundation model to predict clinical trial eligibility from anonymized patient feeds. This model is trained on their licensed real-world data, with a follow-on using clinical trial data.

Subsequent partnerships will leverage previous models, training on additional organization's data. Once we have trained on a plurality of data, our foundation model will be the most performant, and every biomedical AI application in oncology that does not leverage our model will suffer performance deficits in any task.

## Slide 18: Technical strategy
# Technical strategy
### Our technical strategy is a data strategy

Our phase 1 technical strategy is guided by the observation that scale and quality of data drive AI performance in every domain. Therefore, we focus our creative effort on quality partnerships with the best data sources in biomedicine. We are fast-followers of foundation model architectures, training recipes, and other strategies. This affords us lower research costs and faster development times. Furthermore, until we solve data-siloing, the bleeding edge of closed-source research will not be relevant.

In phase 2, our strategy shifts to becoming the dominant player for heterogeneous multimodal data, period. By 2025 most of the world's data will be biomedical and our capabilities must grow. By this point, the talent pool for multimodal foundational models will have both grown and have become bored with LLM's.

In both phases, we will leverage existing LLM's as seeds. We firmly believe beginning with existing models trained on the world's textual data offer the most effective way to incorporate other data sources and modalities.

The earliest models ingest tables as text. We have previously published this work at ACL, a Neurips-level top 5% AI conference.
