## Testing and Acceptance

*   **General.** Implementation and testing of each SaaS Solution may be accomplished in phases or in accordance with any other mutually agreed methodology specified in the applicable Order. The terms of this section shall be applied in accordance with such mutually agreed methodology.

*   **Test Materials.** In accordance with the responsibilities allocated to each party in the applicable Order, the parties shall develop a test plan and appropriate test materials for the SaaS Solution including, without limitation, test scripts, test scenarios, and test databases.

*   **Pre-Live Testing.** Pre-live testing (“Pre-Live Testing”) shall be performed jointly by the parties in accordance with the mutually agreed responsibilities allocated to each party in the applicable Order and shall commence on the date and shall continue for the period of time specified in the applicable Order (or if none is specified, then for thirty (30) days following the date the SaaS Solution is ready for Pre-Live Testing), subject to any extension as mutually agreed to by the parties. Pre-Live Testing shall involve testing the SaaS Solution in a pre-live environment to ensure it (a) possesses the functional capabilities described in the Documentation; (b) conforms to the warranties set forth in this Agreement; and (c) conforms to any other requirements that may be specified in the applicable Order (collectively, the “Testing Criteria”). If Takeda discovers any failures to comply with the Testing Criteria (each, a “Deficiency”) during Pre-Live Testing, Takeda shall report such Deficiencies to Company, and Company promptly shall correct such Deficiencies. Subject to the further terms of this subsection, the process described herein shall repeat as often as is necessary until all Deficiencies identified during Pre-Live Testing have been corrected by Company. If Company fails to correct all Deficiencies within thirty (30) days following the date specified for the completion of Pre-Live Testing in the applicable Order (including any Project Schedule), then such failure shall constitute a Company Event of Default (which Event of Default shall not be subject to a cure period) and Takeda shall have the right to terminate this Agreement, including any Order, in whole or in part, upon written notice, and receive a refund of all Fees paid under the applicable Order.

*   **Production Testing.** Following successful completion of Pre-Live Testing, Takeda will instruct Company to deploy the SaaS Solution in production (“Production” or “Production Use”). During the period of time specified in the applicable Order (or if none is specified, then for thirty (30) days) following the first day of Production Use (the “Production Use Period”), Company shall correct any residual Deficiencies that were not resolved during Pre-Live Testing and any Deficiencies that arise after the SaaS Solution is put into Production. If Takeda discovers any Deficiencies during the Production Use Period, Takeda shall report such Deficiencies to Company, and Company promptly shall correct such Deficiencies. The process described herein shall repeat as often as is necessary until all Material Defects have been corrected by Company and the SaaS Solution has operated in Production without any Material Defects for at least fifteen (15) consecutive days (and, if necessary, the Production Use Period shall be extended until the SaaS Solution has operated in Production without any Material Defects for a period of fifteen (15) consecutive days), provided that Company shall remain obligated to correct all Deficiencies reported by Takeda in accordance with its Support Services obligations.

*   **Project Acceptance.** “Project Acceptance” shall occur when each SaaS Solution made available for access and use under the applicable Order has been fully deployed (including, if applicable, at all facilities and/or in respect of all project phases) and has successfully completed the testing processes described in this section. Nothing else, including Takeda’s use of the SaaS Solution, or any component thereof, in a live, production environment, shall constitute acceptance or affect any rights and remedies that may be available to Takeda under Applicable Laws. If Company fails to achieve Project Acceptance within thirty (30) days following the date for Project Acceptance set forth in the applicable Order (including any Project Schedule), then such failure shall constitute a Company Event of Default (which Event of Default shall not be subject to a cure period) and Takeda shall have the right to terminate this Agreement, including any Order, in whole or in part, upon written notice, and receive a refund for all Fees paid under the applicable Order. Takeda shall have no obligation to pay the Fees under the applicable Order until Project Acceptance. Project Acceptance for any SaaS Solution shall not limit the obligations of Company with respect to any warranty or SLA under this Agreement.

*   **Testing of Enhancements.** Whenever an Enhancement is made available for access and use by Takeda as further described in Exhibit C, Takeda shall have the right to test such Enhancement in accordance with Takeda’s customary testing methodologies, which may include all or a Takeda-determined portion of the methodologies and procedures described in this subsection.

## Data Access, Back-Up and Recovery

*   **Access.** Company agrees that Takeda shall have an ongoing right to access the Takeda Data at any time and to download all or any portion of the Takeda Data at any time.

*   **Back-Up and Retention.**

    *   Company shall make a backup copy of the Takeda Data stored by Company or its Company Agents as often as is necessary to enable compliance with the RPO SLA set forth in Exhibit D and shall make a daily backup copy of such data and store it at a secure, offsite location for at least two (2) weeks.
