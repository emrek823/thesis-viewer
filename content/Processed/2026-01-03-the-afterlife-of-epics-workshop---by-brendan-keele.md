It remains strategically unclear to me why they allow Workshop to persist! It‚Äôs a blemish on their already less-than-stellar record of handling external communications. Moreover, it‚Äôs a continued risk as antitrust and information blocking lawsuits continue to pile up.

More tactically, they are already clearly moving prior Workshop work into the ‚ÄúUnder Construction‚Äù moniker. Look no further than Press Ganey‚Äôs page:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4KJl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e454964-aa01-4aa9-a102-3c2a4ac1976c_1512x1134.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4KJl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e454964-aa01-4aa9-a102-3c2a4ac1976c_1512x1134.png)

Overall, the total number of Workshop apps dropped from 5 down to 4 (seemingly as one of the vendor consolidated their Workshop apps). Perhaps Epic is locked into Workshop by contract and plan to sunset the categories as those term out. Regardless, I‚Äôm confused by contradiction of the admission in October and the laggard behavior here. It‚Äôs distinctly not a good look to only sunset the ambient scribe category after [announcing your own](https://healthapiguy.substack.com/p/epics-ambient-bombshell?utm_source=publication-search)!

One other dynamic of the Showroom marketplace that has me befuddled is what‚Äôs going on with Toolbox. By all accounts, it‚Äôs been a success, offering a path with guardrails and real support to third parties. Developers have shown a continued appetite for those offerings - it‚Äôs grown from 44 entries in July to 78 to close the year.

I started tracking the growth of Toolbox in summer 2024 with [periodic posts](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendan-keeler_took-a-peek-at-the-epic-toolbox-categories-activity-7314667490343624704-mfbA/). Epic made substantial investments since then, with the 21 categories ballooning to 48 as of this past July, as seen in the tracker [here](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1QGi_MT96_IGoji9ESZU95FPNsMiZbSkh_H7Bv9e4GUU/edit?usp=sharing). But the category growth stalled in Q3 and Q4, adding only one by September and none in the final three years.

I do wonder if it ties to the [Texas‚Äô antitrust case against them](https://healthapiguy.substack.com/p/dont-mess-with-texas-epic-edition) and, more specifically, the [Civil Investigative Demand](https://healthapiguy.substack.com/p/epics-two-front-war). From a timing perspective, a CID would undoubtedly have been in progress by July (or possibly even the start of the year). Perhaps the looming crystallization of Texas‚Äô foreclosure theory caused Epic‚Äôs lawyers to freeze things a bit?

All that analysis pales in comparison, though, to the purge we‚Äôve seen in Toolbox since the turn of the year, as the total number of apps dropped from 78 down to 58. Numerous applications were dropped from Toolbox to Connection Hub, such as:

* AvaSure Intelligent Virtual Care Platform
* Becklar Workforce Safety - WorkerSafety Pro
* hellocare.ai
* Imprivata Identity Verification for MyChart
* Insight Health AI Scribe
* Teladoc Solo Inpatient
* Vouched Identity
* VSTOne: AI Care Platform

Some of these appear to correspond with categories that were Under Construction, like MyChart Identity Verification or Staff Duress. Perhaps those vendors failed to participate fully in the Toolbox category's development. But other entries like hellocare.ai or Insight Health were using mature Blueprints. My only guess is that they failed to attain actual clients within a specified time period.

It‚Äôs tough to find the unifying thread here. Nothing about the last six months of Showroom behavior feels like ordinary product management. But it does feel like a marketplace being slowly reshaped by a legal ghost hand that never appears in release notes. Maybe the pattern suggests not indecision, but de-risking: an EHR ecosystem learning, in real time, which parts of itself it can no longer afford to grow.
